Residenza Carducci is a family-owned four story house, substantially renovated in 1556 with parts dating from the 14th century. The building is protected by the National Heritage and has been recently renovated both internally and on the back courtyard. It was one of the first inns in Verona, run by the current owner's grandparents.

The two suites are on the first floor, independent but adjacent, separated by the laundry room. The top two floors are occupied by the owners. The ground floor, formerly the inn's restaurant, now consists of a small courtyard, office, vaulted entryway and garage area.

Suite Nord

The suites are located in the historical part of town called Veronetta, on the left side of the Adige river. Veronetta is where the first settlements of the city were and is the home of the roman theatre, the Giardino Giusti and Castel San Pietro.

Residenza Verazzano

Residenza Verazzano is a large and modern 3 bedrooms apartment located just outside the walls of the ancient city of Verona, on the top story of a building in a quiet and well-served residential quarter called “quartiere catena”.

This quarter takes its name from a tradition started by the Scaligeri family who, here, erected a tower in the center of the Adige river to collect taxes and duties by pulling a chain across the river and blocking entry to boats which, coming from the north, entered the center of the city to carry goods to warehouses and to continue down the river to the Adriatic sea.

We can still see this tower in the center of the Adige when we enter into Verona.
